XIRR guide

Calculate SIP XIRR, final amount, SIP amount or duration

This XIRR calculator helps estimate annualized returns for SIPs, mutual funds and other investments where cashflows happen over time. You can enter any three values: regular investment amount, duration, XIRR and final amount. The calculator solves the missing fourth value using the selected payment frequency and timing.

What is XIRR?

XIRR is the annualized return for cashflows that happen on different dates. It is useful for SIPs because every monthly or yearly investment gets a different amount of time to grow.

XIRR vs CAGR

CAGR is useful when there is one starting amount and one ending amount. XIRR is better for SIP and mutual fund returns because money is invested in multiple installments.

Monthly and yearly SIP support

Select monthly or yearly frequency and choose whether payments happen at the start or end of the period. Those assumptions can change the final amount or XIRR result.

Questions

XIRR Calculator FAQ

How do I calculate XIRR for SIP returns?

Enter your SIP amount, duration and final amount, then leave the XIRR field blank. The calculator generates dated cashflows and estimates the annualized XIRR.

Can I calculate final amount if I know SIP and XIRR?

Yes. Enter the SIP amount, duration and expected XIRR, then leave final amount blank. The calculator estimates the final amount using the selected monthly or yearly schedule.

Does monthly or yearly payment frequency affect XIRR?

Yes. XIRR depends on payment dates. Monthly and yearly contributions create different cashflow dates, so the result can change even when the visible amount and duration look similar.

Is this calculator investment advice?

No. It is a planning tool for estimates only. Actual investment returns depend on market performance, fees, taxes and timing.
